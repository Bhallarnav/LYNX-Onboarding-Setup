### Watch first

:::note
### How leads arrive

:::note
Speed to lead is the single most important habit in FUB. A lead that waits 5 minutes is half as likely to pick up. A lead that waits 30 minutes is already talking to someone else.

:::

- Where leads come from
    
    Team leads flow into FUB automatically from your lead sources — website inquiries, Realtor.ca, social media, and any other platforms the team uses.
    
    When one arrives, FUB sends a push notification to your phone and an email.
    
    Both must be on at all times during business hours.
    
- What to do the moment a lead arrives
    1. Call immediately — within 5 minutes
    2. If no answer — send a text right away: *"Hey [name], this is [your name] from Lynx Real Estate — I just saw you were looking at some homes online. Do you have a few minutes to chat today?"*
    3. Set a follow-up task for tomorrow if no response
    
    Do not wait until you "have a moment." The window closes fast.
    
- Self-generated leads — same rules apply
    
    Whether a lead comes from the team or you generated it yourself — they go into FUB and get worked the same way. When adding a lead make sure to add the Source to SOI, you will not be able to change this later. (Contact your Mentor if you do)
    
    No leads managed in your phone contacts, notes app, or memory.
    
    Everything in FUB.

:::note
Your pipeline is only useful if the stages are accurate.

Move people forward when their situation changes — don't leave someone in "New" for three weeks because you haven't called them back.

:::

- Stage definitions
    
    
- The stage mistake most new agents make
    
    Leaving everyone in "New" or "Active" regardless of where the relationship actually is.

### Logging — the non-negotiable habit

:::note
Log every touch. Every call — even a voicemail. Every text. Every showing. Every meaningful conversation.

Future you will thank present you every single time.

:::

- What to log and how
    
    After every call — open the contact in FUB, hit Log Call, write 2–3 lines: what they said, where they're at, what the next step is. Takes 60 seconds.
    
    After every showing — log it under the contact. Note which properties they saw, their reaction, and what they said they're looking for.
    
    After every text exchange — if something meaningful was said, log a note. You don't need to copy every message — just capture the key information.
    
- Why this matters more than you think
    
    Six months from now, a lead you spoke to once will call you back ready to buy.
    
    If you have notes, you'll remember their name, their situation, and what they told you.
    
    If you don't, you'll sound like you have no idea who they are.
    
    Notes are how you make every client feel like they're your only client.

### Tasks — how you never drop a lead

:::note
A lead without a task is a lead you're about to forget.

Every active contact in FUB needs a next step with a date attached.

:::

- How to set a task
    
    Open the contact → Tasks tab → Add Task → write what you need to do and set a date.
    
    Keep it specific: not "follow up" but "call re: financing — she said she'd have pre-approval by Thursday."
    
- The rule before you close FUB each day
    
    Every active lead has a task. No exceptions.
    
    If you can't think of a next step — that's a sign the lead needs to be moved to Nurture or Unresponsive, not left floating.
    
- Working your task list every morning
    
    Open FUB. Go to Tasks.
    
    Every task due today gets done before you do anything else.
    
    This is your morning routine before prospecting, before checking email, before anything.
    
    Your task list is your real to-do list.
    

---

### The FUB daily routine

:::note
Do this every working day — in this order:

1. **Task list first** — complete every task due today before creating new ones
2. **New leads** — call every lead in "New" status before anything else
3. **Log everything** — every call, text, and conversation from yesterday you haven't logged yet
4. **Move stages** — anyone whose situation has changed since yesterday
5. **Set tomorrow's tasks** — before you close FUB, every active lead has a next step with a date

This takes 20–30 minutes. It is the most important 20–30 minutes of your day.

:::

---

### Cheat sheet — FUB in one page

- Print this and keep it at your desk for your first 30 days
    
    **New lead arrives →** Call within 5 min → No answer? Text immediately → Log the attempt → Set task for tomorrow
    
    **Had a real conversation →** Log notes within 10 minutes → Move stage to Active → Set a specific next-step task
    
    **No response after 6 touches over 7 days →** Move to Unresponsive → Add to drip campaign → Don't delete
    
    **Deal goes under contract →** Move stage → Log all key dates (condition removal, possession) as tasks
    
    **Deal closes →** Move to Closed → Set a 30-day follow-up task → Ask for a Google review (Module 5.5)
    
    **Every morning →** Tasks → New leads → Log → Stages → Set tomorrow's tasks
    

---

:::note
**Common FUB mistakes new agents make**

- Not logging calls because "I'll remember it" (you won't)
- Leaving stages inaccurate because moving them takes effort (do it anyway)
- Only checking FUB when they're at their desk (get the app, use the app)
- Setting vague tasks like "follow up" with no date (be specific or it's useless)
- Deleting unresponsive leads (never delete — people come back)
:::
