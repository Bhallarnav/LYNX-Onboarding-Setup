### Canva — your design tool

:::note
You don't need to know graphic design. You need to know where our templates are and how to use them. Everything is already built — your job is to customize, not create from scratch.

:::

- Getting access
    
    Mentor will add you to the Lynx Canva team account. Once you're in, all branded templates will be in the Shared folder. If you don't have access within your first week — Discord Mentor. Do not start creating content from a personal Canva account using your own branding.
    
- What's locked vs. what you can change
    
    **Locked — do not touch:**
    
    - Lynx logo placement and size
    - Brand colours (the specific hex codes used in our templates)
    - Brand fonts — the typefaces used in headings
    - Any legal or contact disclaimers
    
    **Yours to customize:**
    
    - Property photos
    - Property details — address, price, beds, baths
    - Your name, headshot, and direct contact info
    - Body copy and captions
    - Market stats and neighbourhood information
    
    If you're not sure whether something is locked — don't change it. Ask Mentor first.
    
- The templates you'll use most
    - **Just listed** — for new listings going live
    - **Just sold** — post within 24 hours of a deal closing (get client permission first)
    - **Market update** — monthly stats, neighbourhood insights
    - **Open house** — date, time, address, your contact info
    - **Testimonial** — client review formatted for social (always get written permission)
    - **Personal branding** — introduction posts, milestone posts, community content
- Exporting for social media
    
    Download as PNG for Instagram and Facebook. Download as MP4 if you've built an animated graphic. File sizes matter on Instagram — if a post looks blurry, re-export at higher quality. Always preview before posting — check that your name is spelled correctly, the price is right, and nothing is cut off on mobile.

### Social media — what to post and how often

:::note
Social media is a long game. You're not posting to get immediate leads — you're posting so that when someone in your network decides to buy or sell, your name is the first one that comes to mind because they've been seeing your content for months.

:::

- How often to post
    
    Aim for 3–4 times per week when you're starting out. Consistency matters more than volume — posting every day for two weeks then going quiet for a month does more damage than posting twice a week all year. Pick a cadence you can actually sustain and stick to it.
    
- Content that works — post these
    - **Market updates** — what's happening in Calgary right now, what it means for buyers and sellers. Use CREB stats. Post monthly at minimum.
    - **Just listed / just sold** — every listing and sale you're involved in. These are proof of work.
    - **Neighbourhood content** — restaurants, parks, community events in the areas you work. Shows you actually know the market.
    - **Educational content** — “what does possession date mean,” “how does a condition work,” “what to expect at a home inspection.” Your sphere doesn't know this stuff — teach them.
    - **Personal posts** — who you are outside of real estate. People hire people they like. Show some personality.
    - **Client wins** — with written permission, share the story. Not just “just sold” — the actual journey, what made it special.
- Content that hurts you — avoid these
    - Anything political — full stop, no exceptions
    - Negative comments about other agents, brokerages, or listings
    - Guaranteed results or income claims — *“I can sell your home in 10 days”* is a RECA compliance issue
    - Overposting listings without any personality or value — you'll look like a billboard, not a person
    - Reposting content from other agents without credit
    - Anything you wouldn't want a client, Mentor, or RECA to see
- The platforms that matter in Calgary real estate right now
    
    **Instagram** — most important for building a personal brand. Stories for day-to-day, feed posts for polished content.
    
    **Facebook** — still relevant for reaching homeowners and older demographics. Good for neighbourhood groups and community pages.
    
    **LinkedIn** — underused by most agents. Strong for referral relationships, professional network, and sphere of influence from former careers.
    
    **TikTok / Reels** — high reach potential but requires consistent video content. Don't start here unless you're committed to video. One platform done well beats four done poorly.

### Your first 30 days of social content

:::note
Don't stare at a blank screen wondering what to post. Here's your first month — one post per week minimum, all using Lynx Canva templates.

:::

- Week 1 — Introduce yourself
    
    Post a personal introduction. Who you are, why you got into real estate, what you love about Calgary, and that you're now with Lynx Real Estate Group. Keep it genuine — not a resume, not a sales pitch. A photo of you (not a headshot, something natural) performs better than a graphic for this one.
    
    Caption direction: *“I just joined the team at Lynx Real Estate Group and I couldn't be more excited. [1–2 sentences about why you got into real estate and what you care about]. If you're thinking about buying or selling in Calgary — I'd love to be your person. DM me or grab my number in my bio.”*
    
- Week 2 — Calgary market update
    
    Pull the latest CREB stats. Use the market update Canva template. Keep the caption simple — what the numbers mean for buyers and sellers right now in one or two sentences. Don't just post the graphic with no context.
    
- Week 3 — Educational post
    
    Pick one thing buyers or sellers commonly don't understand and explain it simply.
    
    Ideas: what a condition means and why it protects you, what to expect on possession day, the difference between list price and sale price, what a CMA is and why it matters.
    
    Use a simple graphic or just a photo of you with text in the caption.
    
- Week 4 — Neighbourhood feature
    
    Pick a neighbourhood in Calgary you know well or work in. Share 2–3 things that make it a great place to live — a restaurant, a park, a community feature. Tag local businesses if you can.
    
    This kind of content gets shared and reaches people outside your existing network.

### Getting content approved before you post

:::note
For your first 30 days — run any post that mentions Lynx, uses our branding, or makes any kind of market claim by Mentor before it goes live. This isn't about micromanaging your content — it's about making sure your first impression in the market is a strong one.

:::

- How to get a post approved quickly
    
    DM Mentor in Discord with the graphic and your planned caption. He'll turn it around fast.
    
    After 30 days of posting, once you have the feel for what works and what doesn't — you won't need to check in on every post. Use the first month to calibrate.

### Quick reference — before you post

:::note
Run through this before every post goes live:

- [ ]  Is the Lynx branding correct — logo, colours, fonts untouched?
- [ ]  Is every detail accurate — price, address, spelling of your name?
- [ ]  Does this post add value or just take up space in someone's feed?
- [ ]  Would you be comfortable if your Mentor, a client, or RECA saw this?
- [ ]  If it features a client — do you have their written permission?
- [ ]  Have you previewed it on mobile before posting?

If you can check every box — post it.

:::

---

:::note
**Common marketing mistakes new agents make**

- Creating content from a personal Canva account instead of the Lynx team account
- Posting inconsistently — a burst of activity then weeks of silence
- Only posting listings — no personality, no value, no reason to follow
- Writing captions that are all about you instead of useful to the reader
- Not asking for permission before posting a client's home or story
- Waiting until everything is "perfect" before starting — done is better than perfect
:::
