## How we handle documents at Lynx

:::info
🎯 Everything is digital. No printing, scanning, or emailing PDFs back and forth. If a client asks to sign a paper copy, have the conversation about why digital is faster, safer, and easier for them. In almost every case they'll agree once you explain it.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>The documents you'll send most often</strong></summary>

- Buyer representation agreement — before you show property to any buyer
- Residential purchase contract — every offer you write
- Condition removal — when financing or inspection conditions are satisfied
- Listing agreement — when a seller agrees to list with Lynx
- Amendment to contract — when anything in an accepted offer changes
- Mutual release — when a deal falls apart and both parties need to formally exit

You won't send all of these in your first month. Know they exist and know where to find blank copies — ask Kyle or your mentor for the template folder location.

</details>

## Sending documents for e-signature

:::info
🎯 Before you send anything for signature, read it yourself first. Every field filled in correctly, every blank accounted for, every date accurate. A document with errors that gets signed is worse than a document that gets sent back for corrections.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>Before you hit send — the pre-send checklist</strong></summary>

- [ ] Every field is filled in — no blanks that should have something in them
- [ ] Client name is spelled correctly and matches their legal name
- [ ] Property address is accurate — including suite number if applicable
- [ ] Dates are correct — offer date, condition dates, possession date
- [ ] Price is written correctly in both numeric and written form if required
- [ ] Your name, brokerage, and contact info are correct
- [ ] You've read through the full document once as a final check

If anything feels off, stop and call your mentor or Mitch before sending.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>How to send for signature</strong></summary>

Your e-signature platform will be set up by Kyle during your first week. The general process across most platforms:

1. Upload the completed document
2. Add signers — name and email for each party required to sign
3. Place signature fields on the correct lines
4. Add your own signature field if required
5. Add a personal message in the email — don't send a cold automated email to a client signing a major document
6. Send and confirm they received it

Ask Kyle for the specific platform walkthrough for the tool Lynx uses.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>The personal message that matters</strong></summary>

Never send a signature request cold. Always send a text or call first:

> "Hey [name], I'm sending over the [document name] now for your e-signature — it'll come from [platform name] to your email. Should take about 2 minutes to sign. Let me know if you have any questions before you sign or if you don't see it in your inbox."

This prevents the document from sitting unsigned because they thought it was spam, and it shows you're on top of every step.

</details>

## Tracking and following up on unsigned documents

:::info
🎯 A document sent is not a document signed. Until you have a completed signature on every required line, the deal is not locked. Track everything.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>How to track signing status</strong></summary>

Every e-signature platform shows you real-time status — sent, opened, signed, completed. Check it. If a document has been opened but not signed after a few hours, follow up. If it hasn't been opened after a few hours, call your client. Don't assume they saw it.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Following up without being annoying</strong></summary>

**Same day — 3–4 hours after sending:**

> "Hey [name] — just checking you got the document okay? It should be in your inbox from [platform]. Let me know if you need me to walk you through it."

**Next morning — if still unsigned:**

Call. Don't text. A phone call communicates urgency better than a text and gets resolved faster.

> "Morning [name] — just wanted to make sure you got the signing email, sometimes these go to spam. Do you have 2 minutes to take care of it now while I have you?"

**If there's a deadline attached — be explicit:**

> "Just a heads up — we need this signed by [time] today for the offer to go in on time. Can you take care of it in the next hour?"

Be direct. Clients appreciate knowing there's a real deadline.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What to do if a client can't figure out the e-signature platform</strong></summary>

Walk them through it on the phone. Screen share if needed. Never let a tech issue kill a deal — if the platform isn't working for them, get Kyle involved immediately. There is always a workaround.

</details>

## Storing and organizing completed documents

:::info
🎯 A completed document that nobody can find is as good as a lost document. File everything immediately after signing is complete — not at the end of the week, not when you get around to it. Now.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>File naming — use this format every time</strong></summary>

`YYYY-MM-DD_LastName_DocumentType`

Examples:

- `2025-03-14_Johnson_BuyerRepAgreement`
- `2025-03-22_Johnson_PurchaseContract_123MainSt`
- `2025-04-01_Johnson_ConditionRemoval`

Consistent naming means you can find any document for any client in under 30 seconds. Inconsistent naming means you're searching through folders in a panic while a client waits on the phone.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Where completed documents are stored</strong></summary>

Ask Kyle for the exact folder structure Lynx uses. Every brokerage organizes files slightly differently. What matters is that you use the system, not your own, from your very first deal. Don't create a parallel filing system on your personal computer or desktop.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>How long documents need to be kept</strong></summary>

RECA requires that transaction records be kept for a minimum of 3 years. Your brokerage handles the official record-keeping — your job is to make sure every completed document gets into the system immediately so nothing falls through the cracks.

</details>

## When something goes wrong with a document

:::info
🎯 Wrong price, wrong date, wrong name, missing signature, sent to the wrong email — document errors happen. What matters is how fast you catch them and fix them.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>Caught the error before it was signed</strong></summary>

Void the document in the e-signature platform, correct the error, re-send. Text your client to let them know:

> "I just sent you an updated version — please sign the new one and ignore the previous email."

Simple.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Caught the error after it was signed</strong></summary>

Call Mitch immediately. Depending on what the error is, the fix might be an amendment, a new document, or something more involved. Do not try to figure this out on your own. Document errors in real estate contracts have legal implications — get experienced eyes on it fast.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Sent to the wrong email</strong></summary>

Void the document immediately in the platform, confirm the correct email with your client, re-send. Let Mitch know it happened. If the wrong recipient opened and viewed the document, flag it to Mitch right away as it may have confidentiality implications depending on what was in the document.

</details>

## Quick reference — document checklist

:::tip
✅ **Every time you send a document:**

- [ ] Read the full document yourself before sending
- [ ] Every field filled in correctly — names, dates, price, address
- [ ] Call or text your client before the signature request hits their inbox
- [ ] Track signing status — follow up same day if unsigned
- [ ] File the completed document immediately using the correct naming format
- [ ] Log it in FUB under the client's contact record
:::

---

:::note
💡 **Common document mistakes new agents make**

- Sending documents without reading them first
- Not following up when a document sits unsigned
- Filing documents inconsistently or not at all
- Using personal storage instead of the Lynx filing system
- Trying to fix a post-signature error without calling Mitch first
- Sending a signature request with no personal heads-up to the client
:::
