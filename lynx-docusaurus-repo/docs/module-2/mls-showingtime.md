### Running a buyer search

:::note
A good buyer search takes 5 minutes to set up and saves your client hours of scrolling through properties that don't fit. Get the filters right from the start.

:::

- The filters that actually matter
    - **Location** — neighbourhood, quadrant, or community. Be as specific as your buyer's brief allows. Don't cast so wide that you're sending them 40 listings a day.
    - **Price range** — use their pre-approval ceiling, not their "dream" number. Add 5–10% above ceiling so they see what's available just over their budget.
    - **Property type** — detached, semi-detached, townhouse, condo. Confirm this in your buyer consultation — don't assume.
    - **Beds and baths** — minimum requirements only. Let them tell you what they'd compromise on.
    - **Status** — Active only for live searches. Add Pending if your buyer wants to know what's moving.
- Filters to ignore for most buyers
    
    Days on market, list date, MLS number — these are research tools for you, not search filters for a client auto-alert. Keep client searches clean and relevant.
    
- How many results is too many?
    
    If a search returns more than 15–20 active listings, it's too broad. Tighten the criteria.
    
    Sending a buyer 40 listings a week trains them to stop opening your emails. Curated beats comprehensive every time.

### Saving a search & setting up auto-alerts

:::note
Auto-alerts mean your client hears about new listings from you — not from [Realtor.ca](http://Realtor.ca) or Zillow.

Set these up at or right after the buyer consultation. Never later.

:::

- How to save a search and send it to a client
    1. Run your search with the right filters
    2. Click Save Search — name it something clear (e.g. "Sarah — NW Calgary detached $600–$700k")
    3. Link it to the client's contact record in MLS
    4. Set alert frequency — daily for active buyers, weekly for nurture buyers
    5. Send them the first batch manually with a personal note — don't just let the auto-email fire cold
- The personal note that makes a difference
    
    When you send the first set of listings, text or email your buyer:
    
    *"Hey [name], just set up your search — these are the ones that match what we talked about. Let me know if any catch your eye and we'll book showings. I'll send updates as new ones hit the market."*
    
    Takes 30 seconds. Makes you look attentive rather than automated.

### Reading a listing like an agent

:::note
Buyers read listings for photos and price. Agents read them for information.

Train yourself to see what your client can't — that's how you add value every single time you walk into a property.

:::

- The numbers that tell the real story
    
    **Days on market (DOM)** — how long it's been listed. Under 14 days in a normal market means it's priced well or generating interest. Over 45 days — there's a reason. Find out what it is before you show it.
    
    **Price history** — has it been reduced? How many times? By how much? A listing that's dropped $30k twice is a seller who needs to move. That's negotiating information.
    
    **List-to-sale ratio for the area** — pull recent solds in the same neighbourhood and compare list price to sale price. If homes are selling at 98% of list, a lowball offer won't land. If they're selling at 94%, there's room.
    
    **Original list date vs. current** — sometimes a listing gets cancelled and relisted to reset the DOM clock. Check if the address has appeared before under a different MLS number.
    
- Reading the remarks section
    
    Agent remarks tell you things the public description doesn't — possession flexibility, whether there are offers already, if the seller is motivated, chattels included or excluded, access instructions.
    
    Read them every time. They're where deals get made or missed.
    
- Red flags to know before you show
    - "Sold as is" — ask why before you book
    - Very recent permit history — what was being fixed?
    - Multiple relists in the last 6 months — what keeps falling through?
    - Price significantly below neighbourhood comps — find out why before your client falls in love with it

### ShowingTime — booking & managing showings

:::note
ShowingTime is how you request access to listed properties. It keeps everything organized and creates a paper trail.

Use it for every showing — never try to arrange access directly with a listing agent unless ShowingTime is down.

:::

- Booking a showing
    1. Find the listing in MLS
    2. Click the ShowingTime button on the listing
    3. Select your preferred time — offer 2–3 options if the listing has restricted showing times
    4. Add your client's name and confirm
    5. You'll get a confirmation once the listing agent or seller approves
    
    Book showings at least 2–3 hours in advance when possible. Same-day requests are sometimes declined — especially on occupied listings. Give yourself and the seller time.
    
- Managing a showing day
    - Confirm all showings the morning of — a quick text to your client with the address list and order
    - Build in 20–30 minutes per property minimum — rushing makes buyers anxious
    - If a showing gets declined or cancelled last minute — Slack the team, sometimes another agent can help cover or has a workaround
    - Always leave properties exactly as you found them — lights off, doors locked, lockbox secured
- Leaving feedback after showings
    
    ShowingTime will prompt you to leave feedback after every showing. Do it — listing agents share this with sellers and it builds goodwill in the agent community.
    
    Keep it honest and professional. Two or three sentences is enough.
    
- Handling showing requests on Lynx listings
    
    When we have an active listing, showing requests come through ShowingTime to you (or to whoever is managing the listing).
    
    Confirm or decline promptly — within 1 hour during business hours.
    
    If the seller needs notice, make sure their showing instructions are set up correctly in MLS so buyers' agents know what to expect.

### Quick reference — showing day checklist

:::note
**Before every showing day:**

- [ ]  All showings confirmed in ShowingTime
- [ ]  Client confirmed via text — address list and order sent
- [ ]  Each listing researched — DOM, price history, remarks read
- [ ]  Property notes prepared — 2–3 things to point out at each one
- [ ]  Debrief questions ready — what to ask your buyer after each showing

**After every showing day:**

- [ ]  Feedback submitted in ShowingTime for each property
- [ ]  Buyer debrief logged in FUB — their reactions, what they liked, what they didn't
- [ ]  Next step set — follow-up task in FUB with a date
:::

---

:::note
**Common MLS & ShowingTime mistakes**

- Setting buyer searches too broad and overwhelming clients with listings
- Not reading the remarks section before a showing
- Forgetting to submit ShowingTime feedback (it matters more than you think)
- Booking showings without confirming the client first
- Not checking DOM and price history before advising on offer strategy
:::
