---

:::warning
📞 The listing agreement is a contract. The communication cadence is the relationship. Sellers who feel informed and respected stay calm, stay rational, and make better decisions. Sellers who feel ignored get anxious, second-guess everything, and blame their agent when things get hard.
:::

:::tip
💡 **The non-negotiable: weekly contact, no exceptions**

No matter what is or isn’t happening with the listing, you contact your seller every single week. Not every other week. Not “when there’s something to report.” Every week.

If there’s nothing new, say so directly and tell them what you’re watching. Silence is interpreted as neglect — always.
:::

---

## The Weekly Seller Update — What to Say Even When Nothing Is Happening

The hardest update is the one where nothing happened. No showings, no feedback, no interest. That’s exactly when your communication matters most.

Every update follows the same structure:
1. What happened  
2. What the market is doing  
3. What you’re doing  
4. What comes next  

---

:::info
📋 **Call vs. text vs. email**

Weekly updates should be a **call or email — not a text**.

- Email = professional + referenceable  
- Call = best when there’s emotion or real news  
- Text = feels like an afterthought  

When in doubt: call, then follow up with email.
:::

## Feedback from Showings — How to Deliver It Properly

Feedback is **data**, not opinion.

Your job:
- Translate it  
- Identify patterns  
- Explain what it means  

---

### Examples

| Agent Feedback | How You Deliver It |
|------|----------------|
| "Feels outdated" | Buyers are comparing it to updated homes at this price — this is coming up more than once, so it’s affecting perceived value |
| "Price too high" | Consistent theme is pricing relative to competition — buyers are landing below our ask |
| "Loved it but bought elsewhere" | Strong response — we just need the right buyer to see it sooner |
| "Backyard too small" | That’s a fit issue, not a pricing issue |

---

:::tip
✅ One-off vs pattern

- One comment → mention it lightly  
- Repeated feedback → call it a pattern  

“This is the third time we’ve heard this” = action signal
:::

## The Price Reduction Conversation

This should **never be a surprise**.

If you’ve been:
- sharing feedback  
- referencing market data  

Then the reduction becomes logical — not emotional.

---

### When to bring it up

| Trigger | Approach |
|--------|--------|
| 10–14 days, low activity | Soft mention — “I’m watching this closely” |
| Repeated feedback | “This is now consistent market data” |
| New comp sale | “This changes our positioning” |
| 21+ days, no offer | Direct — “We need to talk price” |

---

### Script

“I want to have an honest conversation about where we stand.

We’ve been on the market for [X] days, with [X] showings, and feedback is pointing to [issue].

Looking at the market — [comp data].

My honest read is we’re priced ahead of buyers right now.

I’d recommend adjusting to [price] because [data points].

I know this isn’t ideal — but it’s the best path forward.

What questions do you have?”

👉 Then stop. Let them respond.

---

:::warning
🚨 If they refuse

Log it in FUB:
- what you recommended  
- when  
- their response  

Protects you and creates a record.
:::

## How to Keep a Seller Calm in a Slow Market

A slow market = more communication required.

Do this:
- Name market conditions clearly  
- Show your work  
- Compare to other listings  
- Adjust strategy  
- Stay honest + steady  

---

### When seller calls anxious

“I hear you — I’m watching this closely too.

Here’s what I’m seeing: [market reality]  
Here’s what I’m doing: [actions]  
Here’s the likely timeline: [realistic expectation]

Let’s walk through it.”

## Communicating Urgency Without Panic

Urgency must be:
- Specific  
- Calm  
- Actionable  

---

### Do this

- Explain what changed  
- Give clear action  
- Give timeline  
- Stay calm  

---

### Don’t do this

- Panic language  
- Repeated calls without info  
- Fake urgency  
- Text urgent news  

---

### Example

“Hey [Name], something changed this morning.

[What happened]

Here’s why it matters: [impact]

I recommend [action], ideally before [time].

Can you talk today?”

---

:::tip
📋 Urgency only works if trust exists

Weekly communication builds trust.  
Trust makes urgency land.
:::
