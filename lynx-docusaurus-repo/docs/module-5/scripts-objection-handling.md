title: 5.3 Scripts & Objection Handling
---

Every objection is a question in disguise. Your job isn't to overcome it — it's to understand what's behind it and respond with confidence. Keep responses short. Never argue. Always follow up with a question.

> 📋 **How to use this card**
These are starting points, not monologues. Say the response, then stop. Let them talk. The follow-up question is where the real conversation starts. If you're ever unsure how to handle an objection in the moment — acknowledge it, tell them you want to think about that with them, and call Mitch before your next meeting.
> 

> ⚠️ **One rule for every objection**
Never get defensive. Never push. Acknowledge what they said before you respond. The formula is: validate → reframe → question. If you skip the validate step, you lose them.
>

## Buyer Objections

---

**"The market is too crazy right now — we'll just wait."**

*"That makes complete sense — and honestly a lot of people feel that way. The thing I'd want you to consider is that waiting has a cost too. While you're renting, someone else is building equity. Can I show you what the numbers actually look like for your situation, and then you can decide?"*

Follow with: "What would need to change for you to feel more comfortable moving forward?"

---

**"We want to keep renting for now."**

*"Totally fair. Renting gives you flexibility. I guess the question is — are you renting by choice, or because buying feels out of reach right now? Because those are two different conversations. If it's the second one, I might be able to show you some options you haven't considered."*

Follow with: "What would owning look like for you, ideally?"

---

**"We're going to just look on our own for a bit."**

*"Of course — that's a great way to get a feel for things. Just so you know, working with me costs you nothing as a buyer. I have access to listings before they hit public sites, and I can save you a lot of time. Would it hurt to have a quick 20-minute conversation first, just so you know what's out there?"*

Follow with: "What neighbourhoods or price ranges are you focused on?"

---

**"Can you lower your commission?"**

*"I appreciate you being upfront about that. My commission reflects the full service I provide — negotiation, access, and someone in your corner through what's often the biggest purchase of your life. What I can promise you is that I'll work to get you the best deal possible, which typically saves you far more than any commission difference."*

Follow with: "Is there a specific concern about value that I can address?" — then call Mitch before making any agreement.

---

**"I'll just call the seller's Realtor directly to put in an offer."**

*"You absolutely can do that — but I want you to understand what you'd be giving up. The seller's agent legally represents the seller, not you. There's nobody in that transaction whose job it is to protect your interests, negotiate for you, or flag issues with the property. As your agent, that's exactly what I do — and it costs you nothing."*

Follow with: "What's drawing you to that property? Let's see what I can find out about it."

## Seller Objections

---

**"We want to list it higher and see what happens."**

*"I hear you — and I want to get you the most money too. The risk with going high is that overpriced homes sit, and a home that sits loses perceived value fast. Buyers see the days on market and assume something's wrong. A well-priced home often gets more — not less — because it attracts multiple offers."*

Follow with: "Can I show you what's happened with similar homes that started high in this area?"

---

**"Our neighbour got more for theirs."**

*"That's a fair reference point — and I've looked at that sale. The differences between the two properties actually explain the gap. Let me walk you through the comparables so you can see exactly how I arrived at this number, because I want you to feel confident in whatever we decide."*

Follow with: "Would you like to walk through the CMA together so you can see how each comp was weighted?"

---

**"We're going to try FSBO first."**

*"I respect that — it's your home and your call. What I'd say is this: statistically, FSBO homes sell for less and take longer, and the paperwork alone is a full-time job. I'm not asking you to commit today. Can I stay in touch, and if it's not going the way you hoped, give me a call?"*

Follow with: "What's your timeline for making a decision if things don't move on your own?"

---

**"Another agent said they'd list it for more / less commission."**

*"I'd rather earn your business than buy it. A higher list price or a lower commission only matters if the home actually sells — for the right price, with the right buyer, on your timeline. What I'd ask is: what does that agent's plan look like, and how does it compare to mine?"*

Follow with: "What's most important to you — the number, the timeline, or the experience?" Then call Mitch before adjusting anything on your end.

---

**"We want to wait until spring."**

*"Spring is a great market — but so is right now, and here's why: there's less competition from other sellers in the winter, and serious buyers are always in the market. If your goal is to maximize price, we should talk about what that actually looks like month-by-month, because the best time to list might not be what you'd expect."*

Follow with: "What's driving the spring timeline — is it a life event, or more of a gut feeling about the market?"

## General Objections

---

**"I have a friend / family member in real estate."**

*"That's great — and you should absolutely work with someone you trust. My only question would be: are they the right person for this specific transaction? Expertise in the neighbourhood, the price range, and the type of deal matters a lot. If they check those boxes, go with them. If there's any doubt, I'd love the chance to show you what I bring."*

Follow with: "Have they done many deals in this area / price range recently?"

---

**"We need to think about it."**

*"Of course — this is a big decision and I'd never want you to feel rushed. Can I ask: is there a specific concern I haven't addressed, or is it more about timing? Because if there's something holding you back, I'd rather talk through it now than have it linger."*

Follow with: "What would need to be true for you to feel ready to move forward?"

---

**"We're talking to a few agents."**

*"Smart — you should. This is an important relationship and you want the right fit. All I'll say is: pay attention to who listens more than they talk, who knows this market cold, and who you'd actually want in your corner when things get complicated. That's the agent worth choosing."*

Follow with: "What's most important to you in the agent you choose?" — then show them exactly that.

---

> 💬 **When you're stuck mid-conversation**
It's okay to say: "That's a fair point — let me think about that for a second." A brief pause is professional, not weak. If the objection is something you genuinely can't answer, say "I want to make sure I give you the right information on that — can I follow up with you tomorrow?" Then call Mitch.
