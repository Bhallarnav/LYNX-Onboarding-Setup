## Before you dial — know this

:::info
🎯 The 60 seconds before you call matter as much as the call itself. Check FUB for any notes on this lead, take a breath, and go in with energy. Clients can hear whether you want to be on the phone with them or not.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>What to check in FUB before calling</strong></summary>

- What did they inquire about? Specific property, general area, or price range  
- Have they been in the system before — any previous notes?  
- What time did the lead come in — are you calling within 5 minutes?  
- Any other context — first-time buyer, relocation, specific neighbourhood  

You won’t always have this information. That’s fine. Just don’t call blind if it’s already in FUB.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>The 5 things you must find out on every call</strong></summary>

By the end of every call you need:

- Timeline — when do they want to move?  
- Financing — pre-approved, cash, or need help  
- What they've seen — online vs in-person  
- Motivation — why they’re moving  
- Decision-makers — who else is involved  

If you don’t know these — the call isn’t done.

</details>

## The script — word for word

:::info
🎯 This is not a script to read robotically. It’s a framework. Sound like a confident human, not a call center.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>The opening — first 60 seconds</strong></summary>

“Hi, is this [name]? Hey [name], this is [your name] from Lynx Real Estate — I saw you were looking at homes online. Did I catch you at an okay time?”

👉 Then STOP. Let them respond.

If yes:  
“Perfect — are you just getting a feel for the market or thinking about making a move soon?”

If bad time:  
“No problem — when’s a better time? I can call at [time] or [time].”

👉 Always lock a callback time.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>The discovery</strong></summary>

Let them talk first.

Ask:
- “What’s drawing you to that area?”  
- “Detached or townhouse?”  
- “Seen anything in person yet?”  
- “What’s your timeline?”  

Then financing:
- “Have you spoken to a mortgage broker yet?”

If no:
- “I can connect you with someone great.”

Decision-maker:
- “Is anyone else involved in the decision?”

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>The appointment close</strong></summary>

❌ Don’t ask: “When works for you?”

✅ Do this:

“I’d love to set up a quick 30-min call — does [day] or [day] work better?”

If hesitant:
“Even 15 mins — just to show you what’s out there.”

If they want listings:
“Totally — let me ask 2 quick questions first so I don’t waste your time.”

👉 Then close again.

</details>

## Handling objections on the first call

:::info
🎯 Objections aren’t rejection — they’re hesitation. Stay calm and curious.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>"We're just browsing"</strong></summary>

“Totally fair — most people start there. The market moves fast though, so even a quick chat now helps.”

👉 Then ask timeline and lightly close.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>"We already have an agent"</strong></summary>

“No problem at all — appreciate you letting me know. Best of luck!”

👉 Move on.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>"Just send listings"</strong></summary>

“For sure — can I ask 2 quick questions first so they’re actually useful?”

👉 Then close appointment.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>"Not ready yet"</strong></summary>

“That’s perfect timing to start learning the market.”

👉 Discovery → set follow-up → log in FUB

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>"We want to think about it"</strong></summary>

“Totally — what’s on your mind? I might be able to help now.”

If still no:
- Get email  
- Send listings  
- Follow up in 2 days  

</details>

## After the call — do this immediately

:::info
🎯 The 5 minutes after the call determine everything.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>If appointment booked</strong></summary>

- Send calendar invite  
- Send confirmation text  
- Log in FUB  
- Set reminder  

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>If no appointment</strong></summary>

- Log full notes  
- Set follow-up (2 days)  
- Send listings if promised  

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>The log entry that matters</strong></summary>

Good example:

“Looking for NW Calgary $650–750k, timeline 3 months, pre-approval in progress, wife involved. Sending listings tonight.”

👉 This makes you sound sharp later.

</details>

---

:::note
💡 Common mistakes:

- Talking too much  
- Asking open-ended close  
- Not identifying decision-maker  
- Weak notes in FUB  
- Giving up after 1 objection  
- Calling without context  
:::
