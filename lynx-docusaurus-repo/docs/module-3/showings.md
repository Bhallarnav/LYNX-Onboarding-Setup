:::warning
🔑 Showings are where buyers fall in love — or don’t.

Your job isn’t to sell every house. It’s to help them figure out which one is right and move quickly when they find it.

The agent who just opens doors gets replaced.  
The agent who adds value at every showing gets referrals.
:::

## Before the showing — prep matters

:::info
🎯 Walking into a showing without prep is the fastest way to look like you’re just opening doors.

Five minutes per property = you look like an expert every time.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>What to research before every showing</strong></summary>

For each property:

- Days on market — if over 30, why?
- Price history — reductions?
- Original list date — relisted?
- Remarks — seller motivation, offer timing, flexibility
- Neighbourhood list-to-sale trends
- Any permits or updates

You don’t need to explain all of this upfront — but you must know it.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Logistics — confirm everything</strong></summary>

- All showings confirmed in ShowingTime  
- Client confirmed same morning  
- Access method known (lockbox, tenant, agent)  
- 20–30 min per property  
- Backup property ready  

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What to bring</strong></summary>

- Phone (MLS, ShowingTime, FUB)  
- Notes or note app  
- Business cards  
- Feature sheets (if available)  

</details>

## At the showing — what to do

:::info
🎯 Great agents don’t talk more — they guide better.

Let the home do the work. You provide context.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>At the door — first 30 seconds</strong></summary>

Arrive early. Test access before clients arrive.

Say:
“Take your time — go at your own pace. I’ll point things out as we go.”

Then step back. Let them react first.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Moving through the property</strong></summary>

Follow their pace.

Point out:
- Light (sun direction)
- Storage (closets, pantry, garage)
- Mechanical (furnace, HWT, panel)
- Layout flow
- Surroundings (what’s outside)

Keep it conversational.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What NOT to do</strong></summary>

- Don’t talk constantly  
- Don’t give your opinion unless asked  
- Don’t open things unless allowed  
- Don’t comment on decor  
- Don’t discuss offers inside  
- Don’t check your phone  

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Reading your buyer</strong></summary>

**They love it:**
- Slow down
- Revisit rooms
- Ask practical questions

**They’re unsure:**
- Neutral feedback
- Moving quickly

**They hate it:**
- Silence
- Short answers
- Heading for exit

Use this — don’t force it.

</details>

## The debrief — after every showing

:::info
🎯 The real feedback happens after you leave the house.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>The 3 questions</strong></summary>

1. “What’s your overall gut feeling?”  
2. “What did you like most?”  
3. “What would hold you back?”  

Then:

“Out of 10 — where does this sit?”

👉 This tells you everything.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>After multiple showings</strong></summary>

“If you had to go back to one — which one and why?”

👉 Forces clarity.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>If they love one</strong></summary>

“It sounds like this one stands out — do you want to go back or talk about an offer?”

If hesitant:
“I’m not rushing you — just want you to understand how this type of property moves.”

👉 Inform, don’t pressure.

</details>

## Logging in FUB

:::info
🎯 Your memory is not a system. FUB is.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>What to log</strong></summary>

- Properties shown  
- Reaction to each  
- Likes / objections  
- 1–10 score  
- Any updated criteria  
- Next step  

Log within 1 hour.

</details>

💡 Common mistakes:

- Talking too much  
- No prep  
- Discussing offers inside  
- Skipping debrief  
- Letting momentum die on a good property  
- Weak notes  
- Not submitting feedback
