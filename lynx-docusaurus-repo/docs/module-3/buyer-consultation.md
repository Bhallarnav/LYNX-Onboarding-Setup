---
:::warning
🤝 The buyer consultation is the most skipped and most important meeting in real estate. Agents who skip it lose control of their buyers within two weeks. Agents who run it well close more deals, get fewer surprises, and work with clients who actually trust them.

Never skip this meeting.
:::

## Why this meeting exists

:::info
🎯 Most buyers think they find a home online and just buy it. This meeting replaces that assumption with reality — and positions you as the expert guiding the process.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>What happens when agents skip this meeting</strong></summary>

- Buyers fall in love with homes they can’t afford  
- They don’t understand conditions  
- They call other agents “just to look”  
- They make emotional decisions  
- You lose control of the process  

One 30-minute meeting prevents all of this.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Virtual vs. in-person</strong></summary>

In-person is best when possible — stronger relationship and easier document walkthrough.

Virtual works fine when needed.

👉 Either way — it happens before showings. No exceptions.

</details>

## Setting up the meeting

:::info
🎯 How you set this up signals how you run your business.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>What to send before the meeting</strong></summary>

"Looking forward to our call tomorrow at [time]. I’ll walk you through how the buying process works in Calgary and what to expect at each step."

👉 No attachments. No homework. Keep it simple.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What to prepare</strong></summary>

- Market snapshot / CMA  
- Buyer representation agreement  
- 2–3 example listings  
- Your consultation flow  

</details>

---

## The consultation agenda

:::info
🎯 Always follow this order.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 1 — Their story</strong></summary>

Ask:
- “What’s prompting the move?”  
- “First time or have you bought before?”  
- “What does the ideal home look like?”  
- “What areas are you drawn to?”  

👉 Don’t interrupt. Let them talk.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 2 — Your process</strong></summary>

Explain:
- How showings work  
- How offers work  
- Conditions  
- Timeline  

Check in:
👉 “Does that make sense so far?”

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 3 — Market education</strong></summary>

Show:
- What their budget gets them  
- Active listings  
- Sold data  
- Competition  

👉 Set expectations early.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 4 — Buyer agreement</strong></summary>

Introduce it after trust is built:

“This confirms I’m working for you — your interests, not the seller’s.”

Explain:
- Representation  
- Term  
- Area  
- Compensation  

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 5 — Next steps</strong></summary>

Close clearly:

“I’ll set up your MLS search today. When something stands out, text me and we’ll get in quickly.”

👉 Confirm criteria + book showing if ready.

</details>

---

## Handling buyer agreement pushback

:::info
🎯 Stay calm. Stay confident.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>"Why sign before seeing homes?"</strong></summary>

“It ensures I’m legally representing you — it protects you more than me.”

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>"What if we find something elsewhere?"</strong></summary>

“We’ll handle that case if it happens — this is about proper representation.”

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>"Can we try first?"</strong></summary>

“Totally — let’s finish today, and we’ll get it signed before the first showing.”

👉 If still no → call your mentor. Do not proceed without it.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>"Other agents don’t require this"</strong></summary>

“Then they’re not representing you — just facilitating.”

</details>

---

## Explaining your value

:::info
🎯 Buyers don’t know what you actually do — show them naturally.
:::

You provide:
- Listing access + insight  
- Market interpretation  
- Offer strategy  
- Negotiation  
- Trusted network  
- Guidance through the entire process  

👉 Don’t list — weave it into conversation.

---

## After the consultation

:::info
🎯 What you do next matters.
:::

<details>
<summary><strong>Same day actions</strong></summary>

- Set up MLS search  
- Send listings with note  
- Log everything in FUB  
- Send follow-up text  
- Set follow-up task  

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Strong FUB log example</strong></summary>

“Buyer consult complete — looking NW Calgary $650–750k, timeline 60–90 days, pre-approved, first-time buyers, motivated by school zones.”

👉 This makes you sound sharp later.

</details>
