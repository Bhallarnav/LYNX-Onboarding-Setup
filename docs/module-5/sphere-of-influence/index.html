5.2 Your Sphere of Influence

No lead source will ever outperform the people who already know, like, and trust you. Your sphere isn't a backup plan — it's the foundation of a long-term real estate career.

What a Sphere Is — Your Most Valuable Long-Term Asset​

Your sphere of influence is every person in your life who knows your name. Former coworkers, neighbours, friends from school, family, people you see at the gym, parents from your kids' soccer team, your dentist. Anyone who, if they ran into you tomorrow, would say "hey, how are you?" — that's your sphere.

The reason it matters: people buy and sell homes on average every 7–10 years, and they almost always use an agent they know or were referred to. If you stay consistently in front of your sphere, you will get those calls. If you don't, someone else will.

💡 The long game A well-maintained sphere doesn't just produce business — it produces referrals. One A-tier contact who refers you to two people who each refer you to two more is more valuable than 50 cold leads. This is how top agents build careers that don't depend on the market.

Building Your Database — Getting to 200+ People in FUB in Your First 30 Days​

Your first job is to build your list. Not a mental list — an actual list of names, phone numbers, and emails in Follow Up Boss. The target is 200+ contacts in your first 30 days. This sounds like a lot. It isn't. Most people know 250–300 people when they actually sit down and think about it.

📋 Where to find your 200 Work through these categories one at a time: your phone contacts, Facebook friends, Instagram followers, LinkedIn connections, old coworkers from every job you've had, neighbours (current and past), friends from school or university, family and extended family, people from any club, team, church, or community group, service providers you use regularly (doctor, dentist, trainer, accountant), parents you know through your kids, or parents you know from your friends.

Go through your phone contacts first — it's the fastest source and most people find 80–120 names just there. Then open Facebook. Then LinkedIn. Add everyone you recognize by name. You are not committing to calling all of them — you're building a database you can segment and work strategically.

⚠️ Don't filter while you build The most common mistake is skipping people you think "won't be interested" or "probably won't buy." You don't know that. Add everyone first. You can tier them later. A contact you almost skipped will refer you someone — it always happens.

How to Categorize Your Sphere — A, B, and C Tiers​

Once you have your list, you tier it. This determines how often you reach out and through what channel. Don't overthink the tiers — go with your gut on each person.

TierWho They AreTarget Count
A — Would refer you tomorrowPeople who already trust you, would pick up the phone if you called, and would genuinely refer you to a friend today. Your closest relationships.20–40 people
B — Knows you existPeople who know you and would recognize your name, but you don't have a deep current relationship with. Warm contacts you haven't spoken to in a while.80–120 people
C — Reconnect firstPeople you know but who may not immediately remember the context. Acquaintances, old contacts, people you've lost touch with. Worth reaching out — just warm up first.60–100 people

✅ How to tag tiers in FUB Use the Tags field in each contact record: add "Sphere-A", "Sphere-B", or "Sphere-C." This lets you filter and run action plans by tier. Ask Kyle to walk you through setting up a Smart List for each tier in your first week.

The 36-Touch System — Staying in Front of People Without Being Annoying​

The 36-touch system means reaching each A-tier contact approximately 36 times per year — about 3 touches per month. Before you panic: most of these touches are lightweight. A text, a like on their post, a quick call to check in. You are not calling everyone 3 times a month with a sales pitch. You are staying present in their life.

Touch TypeWhat It Looks Like
Monthly callsOne genuine phone call per month to your closest A-tier contacts. No agenda. Ask what's going on in their life. Real conversation, 5–10 minutes.
Monthly textsA personal text — a meme they'd like, a congratulations, a "thinking of you" after something life-related. Never copy-paste the same text to everyone.
Market updatesA brief monthly market update via email or text. Keep it short: one stat, one sentence of context, a call to action if they know anyone buying or selling.
Social engagementLike, comment on, or react to their posts. This is a touch. It keeps you visible. It takes 10 seconds. Do it intentionally, not passively scrolling.
Pop-bys2–4 times a year, drop something off in person. A small seasonal gift, a market report, a coffee. No appointment needed — just a doorstep moment. These convert.
B + C tier12–18 touches per year. Monthly email or social content, 2–3 personal texts, 1–2 calls. Keep them warm — they become A-tier over time if you work them.

💬 Quick rule Every touch should feel like it came from a person, not a marketing machine. If you wouldn't send it to your best friend, don't send it to your A-tier. The moment your sphere feels like a mailing list, it stops working.

What to Actually Say — Scripts That Don't Feel Like Scripts​

The biggest barrier to sphere outreach is not knowing what to say. Here are the actual words — adapt them to your voice.

Text — First reach-out after going into real estate

"Hey [Name]! Just wanted to reach out — I recently made the jump into real estate with LYNX Real Estate Group here in Calgary and I'm really loving it. I'd never forgive myself if someone in my circle needed a great agent and didn't know to call me. If you or anyone you know ever has questions about buying, selling, or what the market's doing — I'm your person. No pressure at all, just wanted to put it on your radar!"

Use for: A and B tier contacts you haven't spoken to since getting licensed.

Phone call — Monthly A-tier check-in

"Hey [Name], it's [Your Name] — just calling to catch up, no agenda. How's everything going with [thing you know about their life]? ... [Have a real conversation] ... Hey before I let you go — things are pretty busy on the real estate side right now, if you ever hear of anyone thinking about making a move, I'd really appreciate you keeping me in mind."

The ask at the end is optional — skip it if the conversation went personal and it would feel forced.

Text — Market update (casual)

"Hey [Name]! Quick heads up — the Calgary market has been moving faster than a lot of people expected this spring. If you've been thinking about buying or selling (or know someone who is), now's a good time to have a conversation. Happy to chat anytime — even just to give you a sense of what your home might be worth."

Personalize with one real local stat if you have it. Never send this to your whole list at once — batch it by tier over a few days so it reads like a personal text.

Email — Reconnecting with a C-tier contact

Subject: Catching up

"Hi [Name], hope you're doing well! It's been a while since we've connected — I think the last time was [context if you remember]. I recently joined LYNX Real Estate Group and I'm focused on helping people in Calgary buy and sell homes. I'd love to reconnect — no pitch, I just like keeping in touch with good people. If there's ever anything I can help you with on the real estate side, I'm always happy to have a no-pressure conversation."

Keep subject line warm: "Catching up" or "It's been a while!" — avoid anything that reads like a newsletter. You don’t need to include anything Real Estate related, just be you and build a connection, let them ask about it.

DM — Social media, after engaging with their content

"[Genuine comment on their post]. By the way — congrats on [life thing if applicable]! I've been meaning to reach out. I Hope all is well with your life and I love to see you doing great!"

Only send this after genuinely engaging with their post first. This doesn’t need to be Real Estate related, just stay relevant with your social media account and in their brain.

First Month Sphere Outreach Plan​

Don't try to contact 200 people in one sitting. Work the plan below. By the end of week four, every contact in your database will have heard from you at least once.

WeekFocusWhat to Do
Week 1Build your listGet to 200+ names in FUB. Don't reach out yet — just build and tier. Set up your Sphere-A, B, C smart lists with Kyle's help.
Week 2Contact your A-tierCall your 20–40 closest contacts personally. These are people who will be excited for you. Use the phone call script above. These calls will energize you — do them first.
Week 3Text your B-tierSend personalized texts to your B contacts. Do 15–20 per day. Adapt the first-reach-out script to each person's context. Respond to every reply before sending more.
Week 4Email your C-tierSend the reconnect email to C-tier contacts. Batch into groups of 20 over the week. For any C-tier who replies warmly, move them to B and follow up with a text.

✅ End-of-month checkpoint By day 30 you should have: 200+ contacts in FUB tagged by tier, every A-tier personally called, every B and C contacted at least once, and at least 3–5 replies or conversations that showed buying or selling interest. Bring this to your first monthly review with Mitch.

⚠️ The most common mistake Waiting until you feel "ready." There is no ready. You will feel awkward on the first call. Do it anyway. The second call is easier. By week two you'll have found your rhythm. The agents who build strong spheres are not the ones who had a natural gift for it — they're the ones who started before they felt comfortable.
