5.1 How Leads Work on Our Team

Leads are the lifeblood of this business. Understanding how they're assigned, tracked, and protected isn't optional — it's foundational. Read this carefully.

How Team Leads Are Assigned​

When a lead comes in through a team source — whether that's the website, a referral partner, a paid campaign, or any other channel the team has built — it enters Follow Up Boss and gets assigned to an available agent based on rotation and availability status.

This system is only fair if everyone plays by the same rules. Your rotation slot isn't guaranteed — it depends on your responsiveness. If you consistently miss or slow-respond, leads will shift to agents who are active.

📋 The assignment basics Team leads are distributed through Follow Up Boss. You'll receive a notification via the app and email the moment a lead is assigned to you. Log in, review the contact, and make your first attempt immediately.

Leads are assigned based on a few factors: active status in FUB, rotation order, and — over time — your conversion history. Agents who close leads consistently earn more of them. That's not politics, that's how a high-functioning team allocates its best opportunities.

Speed to Lead — What Happens When You Don't Respond Fast​

Speed to lead is the single biggest variable in whether a lead converts or disappears. The data on this is unambiguous: the odds of making meaningful contact drop off sharply after the first five minutes. After an hour, you're often chasing someone who's already booked a showing with someone else.

Time WindowWhat It Means
0–5 minutesLead comes in. You respond. This is the standard. A quick text or call acknowledges the inquiry and sets the tone.
5–15 minutesAcceptable if you were on a call or showing. Respond as soon as you're free. Send a quick text first if you can't call immediately.
15–60 minutesBorderline. If this is a pattern, it will be flagged during your performance review. One-off situations happen — patterns don't.
1+ hourThe lead is now at risk of being reassigned. If it happens more than twice without explanation, it becomes a formal performance conversation with Mitch.
No responseLead is reassigned immediately. This is documented. Three documented instances will result in a review of your lead access privileges.

⚠️ This is where we draw the line Slow response on a team-sourced lead isn't just a missed opportunity for you — it reflects on the team's relationship with that client and the partners who sent them. We protect that reputation. Repeated non-response is a conduct issue, not just a performance issue.

What to say when you can't call immediately:

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with LYNX Real Estate. I just received your inquiry and I'm going to give you a call within the next 15 minutes. Looking forward to connecting."

Self-Generated Leads — How They're Tracked and Treated Differently​

If you generate a lead through your own effort — your sphere of influence, your social media, a door-knocking campaign, a referral from a past personal client — that's yours. It doesn't go through the team rotation. It belongs to you.

That said, it still needs to be tracked. Every lead you work — regardless of source — goes into FUB. This isn't about surveillance, it's about giving you a complete pipeline view and ensuring that if something happens mid-transaction, the team can support you.

✅ How to tag self-generated leads in FUB When entering a self-generated lead, set the Lead Source field to "Agent Personal" This flags it as outside the rotation and ensures it won't be counted against your speed-to-lead metrics if your response window differs. Ask Kyle or Mitch if you're unsure where to find this field.

  • Self-generated leads are yours to own, nurture, and close
  • They still go into FUB — always
  • They are not subject to rotation reassignment
  • Commission splits on self-generated leads follow your individual agreement — confirm with Mitch if you're unsure
  • If you need help converting a self-generated lead, treat it like any other: ask a mentor before you guess

What Happens If a Lead Is Reassigned​

Lead reassignment is not a punishment. It's a protection — for the client, and for the team's reputation. If a lead is reassigned away from you, here's what that means and what comes next.

🔄 Reassignment triggers A lead will be reassigned when: you don't respond within the response window, you indicate you're unavailable and can't be reached, the client specifically requests a different agent, or if you determine the lead is a better fit for another agent's skillset or market area. Also, consider if you’re not following a proper speed to action, you’re taking business away from other team members who need this work too.

When a lead is reassigned, you'll receive a notification in FUB and a direct message from Mitch or Kyle explaining why. This conversation is a coaching moment, not a disciplinary one — the first time. It becomes part of your performance record if it becomes a pattern.

You do not retain any claim to a reassigned lead. Reaching out to that client directly after reassignment is a serious breach of team protocol and will be treated as a conduct issue.

If you know you'll be unavailable:

"Hey Mitch [or Kyle], I have [situation] from [time] to [time] and won't be able to respond to new leads. Can we pause my leads in FUB for that window?"

Proactive communication is always the right move. We work around real life — we just need to know in advance.

How Your Performance Is Tracked and Reviewed​

FUB tracks everything automatically. Response times, contact attempts, stage progression, days in pipeline — it's all visible to Mitch and Kyle in real time. This isn't about micromanagement. It's about knowing where to help you.

MetricStandard
First response time on team leads5 minutes (target)
Minimum contact attempts before marking a lead cold16 minimum attempts
Pipeline review cadenceMonthly
Where all performance data livesFUB — keep it updated

Performance reviews happen monthly. Mitch and/or Kyle will pull your FUB data and walk through your pipeline with you — what's active, what's stalled, what needs a new strategy. These aren't evaluations, they're coaching sessions. Come prepared with questions.

📊 What Mitch and Kyle can see Your response time on every lead. How many contact attempts you've made. How many leads are sitting untouched. Stage progression and days in each stage. Notes and call logs — which means your notes need to be accurate and timely.

  • Log every call, text, and showing in FUB — even if the outcome was nothing
  • Update lead stages as they move — don't let leads sit in "New" for days
  • If a lead has gone cold, mark it accurately and note why
  • Never delete a lead or alter notes to cover a gap — come to Mitch instead
  • Your pipeline is a live document — treat it that way

💬 Quick rule If a lead has had no activity in 72 hours and you haven't flagged it — that's a problem. Either the lead is being worked and FUB doesn't know it, or it's being neglected. Neither is acceptable. Update your notes in real time.
