3.5 Offer to Close

warning

🏁 Getting an offer accepted is just the start.

What happens between acceptance and possession is where deals either hold together or fall apart — and where your clients need you most.

Never assume something is handled until you’ve confirmed it yourself.

Watch first​

tip

📹 [Add your Loom link here] — Walk through the full contract-to-close timeline. Show key dates, responsibilities, and how you communicate throughout. 8–10 minutes.

The moment an offer is accepted​

info

🎯 The first 30 minutes matter most.

What to do immediately
  • Call your buyer (never text)
  • Confirm deposit (due within 24 hours)
  • Notify mortgage broker
  • Book home inspection (same day)
  • Log everything in FUB
  • Set all critical dates as tasks
Send your buyer the timeline

“Here’s what the next few weeks look like:

  • Deposit due: [date]
  • Inspection: [date]
  • Financing deadline: [date]
  • Condition removal: [date]
  • Possession: [date]

I’ll guide you through each step.”

👉 Removes uncertainty immediately.

The deposit​

info

🎯 Typically due within 24 hours in Alberta.

How to explain it

“It goes into the listing brokerage’s trust account — not to the seller.”

Confirm:

  • method
  • timing
  • receipt

Log it in FUB.

If deposit is at risk

Call listing agent immediately.

👉 This is a Mitch situation if unsure.

The home inspection​

info

🎯 Book it SAME DAY.

Your role
  • Attend
  • Stay calm
  • Take notes/photos
  • Ask inspector to clarify severity
How to interpret

3 categories:

  • Safety issues → serious
  • Maintenance → normal
  • Cosmetic → ignore

👉 Help buyer separate emotion from reality.

Next steps
  • Proceed
  • Negotiate
  • Walk away

👉 Buyer decides — you guide.

The financing condition​

info

🎯 Stay proactive — don’t wait.

During financing
  • Check with broker every 2 days
  • Ensure buyer responds quickly
  • Flag issues immediately
If financing fails

Call buyer immediately.

Options:

  • Exit
  • Extension
  • Alternative financing

👉 Call Mitch.

Condition removal​

info

🎯 This is when the deal becomes firm.

How to explain

“Once you sign — deposit is at risk if you walk away.”

Wait for clear confirmation.

If deadline is missed

Deal can collapse.

👉 Call Mitch immediately.

Between removal and possession​

info

🎯 Quiet period ≠ no communication.

What must happen
  • Lawyer engaged
  • Insurance arranged
  • Final walkthrough scheduled
  • Utilities arranged
Weekly check-in

“Everything is on track — let me know if you need anything.”

👉 Prevents anxiety.

Final walkthrough

Confirm:

  • Inclusions present
  • Condition unchanged
  • No damage

If issues → call listing agent immediately.

Possession day​

info

🎯 Keys usually released after funds transfer.

Your role
  • Stay available
  • Communicate updates
  • Call when keys released
  • Meet buyer if possible
Your role is NOT
  • Moving coordinator
  • Legal fixer
  • Locksmith

👉 Escalate when needed.

If something goes wrong

Call listing agent → then Mitch.

Stay calm. Communicate clearly.

After possession​

info

🎯 This is where referrals are built.

Follow-up system

Day 7 → check-in + review ask
Day 30 → follow-up
Month 6 → market update
Month 12 → anniversary call

Review ask

“Would mean a lot if you left a quick review — here’s the link.”

👉 Send via text.

What can go wrong​

note

💡 Common issues:

  • Financing fails
  • Inspection issues
  • Seller delays possession
  • Buyer wants to walk after firm

👉 Every one = call Mitch immediately.

Timeline summary​

tip

✅ Acceptance:

  • Call buyer
  • Deposit confirmed
  • Broker notified
  • Inspection booked
  • Timeline sent

During conditions:

  • Broker follow-ups
  • Inspection attended
  • Issues addressed

Removal:

  • Buyer fully understands
  • Signed + sent

Pre-possession:

  • Lawyer + insurance
  • Walkthrough

Possession:

  • Available
  • Keys confirmed

After:

  • Follow-ups scheduled
note

💡 Common mistakes:

  • Not booking inspection same day
  • Going quiet during conditions
  • Missing deadlines
  • Not sending timeline
  • Trying to solve issues alone
  • Disappearing after possession
