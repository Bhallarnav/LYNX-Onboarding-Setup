3.4 Writing & Presenting an Offer

warning

✍️ Writing an offer is where everything you've learned about your buyer comes together.

A well-written offer:

  • protects your client
  • shows professionalism
  • gives you the best chance of winning

Get your mentor or Mitch to review your first 3–5 offers. No exceptions.

Watch first​

tip

📹 [Add your Loom link here] — Full walkthrough of an AREA residential purchase contract. Go clause by clause, highlight common mistakes, and show how you present it to a buyer. 12–15 minutes.

Before you write anything​

info

🎯 The strategy happens BEFORE the contract.

Never open the paperwork until you know exactly what you're doing.

Pre-offer strategy conversation

Cover:

  • Price — based on comps and DOM
  • Conditions — financing, inspection, risk
  • Possession date — buyer vs seller needs
  • Deposit — amount and timing
  • Inclusions/exclusions — confirm everything
  • Competition — are there other offers

👉 You recommend. They decide.
👉 Log everything in FUB.

Call your mentor (first 5 offers)

Example:

“I’ve got an offer going in on [property]. Buyer approved at $X, planning $Y with financing + inspection. Possession [date]. Anything I should consider?”

10 minutes = months of mistakes avoided.

The contract — what matters​

info

🎯 You don’t need to memorize everything.

You need to get the key sections right.

Parties & property
  • Buyer’s legal name (must match ID)
  • Full property address
  • Price (numeric + written — must match)
  • Deposit (typically 1–3%)

Common mistake: Names incorrect or price mismatch.

Conditions (most important)

Financing

  • Protects buyer
  • Typically 5–7 business days

Inspection

  • Right to walk away
  • Same 5–7 days (run together)

👉 Never push buyers to waive without explaining risk.

Possession date
  • Usually noon
  • Must work for buyer
  • Allow 3–4 weeks minimum

Too tight = problems at closing.

Inclusions & exclusions
  • Appliances, coverings, openers
  • Anything custom → write it

👉 Never assume something stays.

Irrevocability
  • Usually 24–48 hours
  • Must give seller time to review

👉 Don’t set unrealistic deadlines.

Common mistakes​

note

💡 What new agents get wrong:

  • Buyer name incorrect
  • Price mismatch
  • Wrong condition timelines
  • Possession too tight
  • Assuming inclusions

Presenting the offer to your buyer​

info

🎯 Never just send DocuSign.

Walk them through it.

How to present

Explain:

  • Price vs market
  • Deposit
  • Conditions
  • Possession
  • Inclusions
  • Irrevocability

Then ask:

“Any questions before you sign?”

👉 Don’t rush this.

After they sign​

Submitting the offer
  • Send via e-signature
  • Email listing agent
  • Call/text to confirm receipt
  • Confirm with buyer
  • Log everything in FUB
Cover note

“Hi [Name], please find attached an offer on [address].
Irrevocability is [date/time].
Let me know if you have any questions. Thanks.”

👉 Keep it clean. No overselling.

Multiple offers​

info

🎯 Stay calm. Guide clearly.

When competition exists

Call buyer:

“There are [X] offers. Here’s what it means.”

Discuss:

  • Price
  • Conditions
  • Possession
  • Strategy

👉 Give real advice, not comfort.

Escalation clauses

Use only with guidance.

Not always accepted. Can backfire.

If they lose

“You did everything right. Sometimes it comes down to terms.”

Then: 👉 Move forward immediately.

Checklist​

tip

✅ Before writing:

  • Strategy confirmed
  • Mentor consulted
  • Comps reviewed

While writing:

  • Legal name correct
  • Price matches
  • Conditions correct
  • Possession realistic
  • Inclusions confirmed

Before submitting:

  • Walkthrough done
  • Signed
  • Agent notified

After:

  • Buyer updated
  • Logged in FUB
  • Follow-up set
note

💡 Common mistakes:

  • No mentor review
  • Wrong names
  • Wrong timelines
  • Weak explanation to buyer
  • No follow-up with listing agent
  • Rushing submission
