1.5 Team Expectations & Culture

note

This isn't a list of rules to memorize. It's how we operate. The agents who thrive at Lynx aren't just good at real estate — they're good teammates. Read this once, then come back to it if something feels off.

Availability & response times​

note

Real estate moves fast. Clients and teammates need to know they can count on you. You don't need to be available 24/7 — but when you're working, you're responsive.

  • Client response time standard

    Respond to all client calls, texts, and emails within 2 hours during business hours. If you can't talk, send a quick text: "Hey [name], I'm with a client right now — I'll call you back by [time]." That one message prevents 90% of "my agent never calls me back" complaints.

  • Team response time standard

    Respond when you can. If anything is urgent, you will get a call. Call turnaround time should be under an hour.

  • After hours

    You don't have to answer calls at 10pm. Set boundaries that work for you. What's not acceptable is going dark for 12+ hours during a live deal or when a client is expecting to hear from you. Use your judgment — and when in doubt, a 30-second text goes a long way.

Communication — how we talk to each other​

note

Good internal communication prevents 80% of the problems that derail deals and damage client relationships.

  • Discord— what it's for

    Day-to-day team communication, quick questions, tech issues, sharing wins, general updates. Keep it professional but not stiff. Use the right channels — don't DM everything to your Mentor or your Mentor when it belongs in a channel.

  • When Discord isn't enough — pick up the phone

    If something needs more than 3 back-and-forth messages to resolve — call. If it's urgent — call. If it involves a client situation, a contract issue, or anything that could go sideways — call your Mentor directly. Don't let important things die in a thread.

  • How to ask a good question

    Before you message the team with a question, do three things: check the handbook, check if you can figure it out yourself in under 5 minutes, then ask. When you do ask — give context. Not "how do I write a condition?" but "I have a buyer offer going in tonight on a detached in Cochrane, they want a Sewer Scope condition — what's the standard wording we use?" Better questions get faster, better answers.

  • Sharing wins and losses

    When you close a deal — share it in Discord / Google Chat. When something goes wrong and you learn from it — share that too. This team gets better when people are honest about both. Nobody here is judging you for a tough deal. We're all in it together.

Professionalism — in person and online​

note

How you show up reflects on every agent at Lynx. This isn't about being rigid — it's about being someone clients trust the moment they meet you.

  • At showings and appointments

    Arrive early or on time — never late without a heads-up call. Dress like you're already at the level you want to reach. Keep your car clean if you're driving clients. Put your phone away when you're with someone. These things sound small. They're not.

  • Social media — what's okay and what's not

    Post market stats, neighbourhood content, your listings, client wins (with permission), and your personality — that's what builds your brand. Do not post anything political, anything that could embarrass a client, anything that makes negative comments about other agents or brokerages, or anything that makes a representation or guarantee about real estate results. When in doubt — don't post it, or run it by Kyle first.

  • Representing Lynx in the community

    You are Lynx when you're at open houses, networking events, and anywhere you hand out a business card. Be someone you'd want to work with. The Calgary real estate community is smaller than it looks — reputations travel fast in both directions.

Mistakes — how we handle them here​

note

Every agent on this team has made mistakes. The difference between a fixable mistake and a catastrophic one is almost always how fast it gets flagged. We do not have a culture of blame here — we have a culture of fixing things fast.

  • The rule on mistakes

    If something goes wrong — you missed a deadline, you said something you shouldn't have to a client, you made an error in a contract — tell your Mentor immediately. Not tomorrow. Not after you've tried to fix it yourself. Now. The sooner it's on the table, the more options we have to resolve it.

  • What not to do

    Don't hide it. Don't guess your way through a fix. Don't tell the client something that isn't true to buy yourself time. These are the things that turn a recoverable situation into a real problem. One honest conversation with your Mentor is almost always enough to get things back on track.

  • If a client is upset

    Listen first. Don't get defensive. Don't promise things you can't deliver. Say: "I hear you — let me look into this right away and get back to you within the hour." Then call your Mentor. Upset clients can become your biggest advocates if you handle the situation with honesty and urgency. Most of the time they just want to feel heard and know someone is on it.

The standard we hold here​

What we expect is simple: be honest, be responsive, be coachable, and care deeply about the client experience. Skills can be taught. Character and consistency matter more.
